Diskarte now has a definitive technical overview — a single document that explains the whole system end to end, without requiring prior context.

It exists because a serious platform deserves a serious description. Landing pages compress; documentation fragments. Somewhere in between there should be one artifact a CTO, a staff engineer, or a technical investor can read linearly and come away with the complete mental model: what Diskarte is, why it exists, how it works, what has actually been built, and what is deliberately not built yet.

## What it covers

Twelve parts and a diligence appendix:

- **Vision** — why AI made execution fast without making organizations coherent, and what layer is missing.
- **Product** — the surfaces an organization actually touches: the Morning Brief, Organizational Memory, Judgment, governed Practices, onboarding.
- **Runtime and the Constitutional Model** — the intellectual core: kernel primitives, computed authority, and the evidence → truth → canon spine.
- **Greenfield and Brownfield** — the two onboarding paths and the convergence law between them.
- **Foundations, Substrate, and Operations** — distribution without colonization, execution without lock-in, and operating the platform without holding authority inside any organization.
- **Status and roadmap** — the honest ledger, with five status labels used consistently and never blurred.

## How it treats the truth

Every capability claim in the document carries one of five statuses: Vision, Architecturally Complete, Implemented, Deployed (Internal Dev), or Planned. "Implemented" means built, committed, and proven through verification — it does not imply deployment. The document states plainly that zero external organizations have used Diskarte yet, and that production deployment of the managed path is intentionally gated.

That honesty is not a disclaimer section. It is the document's method, and the product's.
